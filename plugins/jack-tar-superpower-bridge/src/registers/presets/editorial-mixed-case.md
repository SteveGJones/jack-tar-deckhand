## Typography

Display: serif with strong contrast and editorial voice (GT Sectra, Tiempos Headline, Newsreader Display) at 32–40 pt for titles, **sentence case** — never all caps. Body: complementary humanist sans (Söhne, Inter, GT America) at 14–17 pt. Pull-quotes get italic display serif at 24–28 pt with the terracotta accent for the quote-marks only. Straps are prose-sentence cadence; this register is the canonical home of the sentence-cadence strap style. Numbers in body copy use proportional figures.

## Layout typology

Defaults to mixed-case body copy with frequent pull-quotes (~9.0 × 1.5 in, banner scale) breaking up dense reasoning. SMARTART-FROM-LIST at the inline scale (~3.0 × 3.0 in) for short evidence clusters; banner scale (~10.0 × 2.0 in) for linear sequences when they aid the argument. IMAGE markers are illustrative — schematic line drawings, hand-drawn-feel diagrams, single-colour editorial illustrations — paired with explanatory captions. Native CHART:slug for any quantitative series with the terracotta accent reserved for the single highlighted data point. BG markers are rare; when present, vellum paper-grain texture only.

## When to reach for this register

Long-form essay decks, position papers, magazine-style strategy talks, op-ed-style framings, and any deck where prose carries weight and visuals support the writing rather than the other way around. Sentence cadence is mandatory — this register's defining choice. Pair with Hero's Journey or Pruning Frame arcs. When the speaker says "I'm writing this for people who read", this is the preset.
