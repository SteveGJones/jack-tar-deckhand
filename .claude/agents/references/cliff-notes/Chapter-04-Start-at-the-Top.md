# Chapter 4: Start at the Top

**Thesis:** Transformation to an AI-First business begins with a top-down enterprise service map -- not technology pilots -- because without strategic visibility, Shadow AI accumulates undetected and governance remediation costs 15-20% of the original uncoordinated spend.

## Key Concepts

### Why Top-Down Discovery Is Non-Negotiable
Bottom-up AI pilots create four failure modes: redundant investment (three departments each building sentiment analysis), contradictory decisions (Sales AI approves customers that Risk AI flags), accountability gaps (no named owner when AI fails), and lack of end-to-end visibility. The high-level mapping requires 2-3 days of aggregate C-suite time but prevents 6-9 months of retroactive governance cleanup. In the Chapter 1 CFO scenario, governance remediation would have consumed $1M-$1.3M of the $6.5M Shadow AI spend.

### The Top-Down Service Lens
Discovery follows a strict sequence: clarify why the enterprise exists, list the services that fulfill that mandate, identify accountable owners, then discuss delivery mechanisms. Facilitation starts by naming strategic outcomes (customer promises, regulatory obligations, operating goals) and drawing 4-8 Level 0 services. AI Personas appear only after a service owner requests help answering the Three Questions of Delegation.

### Executive Sequencing Checklist
A five-step cadence that leaders should hear in every steering session: (1) Affirm the service WHAT using the Level 0 view, (2) State the Persona mission documenting WHAT clauses of the Persona Contract, (3) Name the Domain Boundary so cross-domain SOPs can be flagged early, (4) List required SOPs recording the HOW backlog, (5) Assign policy governance ownership nominating the single policy repository operator.

### Moving Away from "How"
Stakeholders jump to solutions. Redirect platform, prompt, model, and API discussions to downstream chapters. Focus exclusively on three things: what outcomes each service owns, who is responsible (service owner plus potential delegates), and why value is exchanged between services. Processes remain important but derive from architecture, not vice versa.

### AI RM as Mandatory Shared Service
AI Resource Management must appear at Level 0, visible alongside HR, Finance, and Cybersecurity. When AI RM is on the map from day one, service owners remember to involve it during Stage 0 readiness checks, AI Persona templates include onboarding and telemetry details, and governance councils treat AI delegation as enterprise infrastructure rather than optional audit.
